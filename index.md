<div class="prose">
<p>I'm an engineering leader based in Toronto, currently managing a team of performance engineers at <strong>Amazon Web Services</strong> focused on ML workloads for <a href="https://aws.amazon.com/machine-learning/trainium/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">AWS Trainium</a>, Amazon's custom ML accelerator chip. My team benchmarks, profiles, and optimizes large-scale models to get the most out of custom silicon.</p>

<p>Before AWS, I spent five years at <strong>Intel</strong> managing a compiler engineering team that built the <a href="https://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/developer/tools/oneapi/fpga.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener">oneAPI FPGA toolkit</a>. I joined Intel as an individual contributor in 2015, worked on their HLS and OpenCL compilers, and eventually led the team through the design and launch of SYCL HLS. I enjoy working on the interface between hardware and software and helping expose the capabilities of novel architectures to developers in a way that's both powerful and usable.</p>

<p>My academic background is in computer architecture. I did my MASc at the <strong>University of Toronto</strong> under <a href="https://www.ece.utoronto.ca/people/enright-jerger-n/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Prof. Natalie Enright Jerger</a>, where I worked on silicon interposer-based multi-chip systems and networks-on-chip. That work led to publications at MICRO and was selected for <em>IEEE MICRO Top Picks</em>. Before Toronto, I completed my BTech in Electronics &amp; Communications at <strong>IIT Guwahati</strong>.</p>
